Bespoke: how off-market access actually works

Davide Piraldo·July 2026·4 min read
editorial photograph — private entrance, Monte-Carlo

The most sought-after addresses in Monaco rarely appear on a public portal. By the time a listing reaches a website, it has usually already been offered — quietly — to a shorter list of buyers first.

Why sellers keep properties off-market

Discretion is the primary reason. Many owners of Monaco's most valuable residences prefer not to have their sale — or the fact that they're selling at all — visible publicly. A private mandate lets a seller test the market, gauge interest, and negotiate without the property's value, condition or timeline becoming public information.

What a private search mandate actually is

For buyers, the mirror version is a dedicated buying mandate: instead of browsing what's publicly listed, an advisor works your specific brief — size, district, budget, timeline — against the full market, including relationships with owners, notaries and other agents who know about opportunities before they're advertised anywhere.

"A portal shows you what's for sale today. A mandate shows you what will be for sale next month — before anyone else sees it."

Why relationships still outperform portals

Monaco's real estate community is genuinely small. The same handful of notaries, family offices and long-established agencies handle a disproportionate share of the Principality's highest-value transactions. Being inside that network — not just searching within it — is what off-market access actually means in practice.

This is also why "bespoke" isn't a marketing word here so much as a description of process: every mandate is built around one buyer's specific criteria, rather than a buyer sorting through a generic inventory built for everyone.
