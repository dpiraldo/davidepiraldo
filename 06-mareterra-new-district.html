Mareterra: Monaco's new waterfront district

Davide Piraldo·July 2026·5 min read
editorial photograph — Mareterra, Anse du Portier

Monaco cannot expand outward on land — so it expanded into the sea. Mareterra, inaugurated in December 2024 at Anse du Portier, added roughly six hectares of new territory to the Principality: a jump of over 3% in Monaco's total surface area.

The masterplan was entrusted to Renzo Piano Building Workshop, alongside Valode & Pistre and landscape designer Michel Desvigne — the same architectural pedigree behind projects like the Centre Pompidou. Around 110 apartments and 10 villas make up the residential core, deliberately height-limited to preserve sightlines from the older city to the sea.

Why it matters for buyers

Mareterra is the first entirely new residential district Monaco has built in decades — and, for practical purposes, may be one of the last. There is no comparable land available for a second such project in the foreseeable future, which is part of why early Mareterra transactions have already set price records: waterfront penthouses have traded above €130,000 per square metre.

"Mareterra isn't just a new address — it's the only new land Monaco is likely to create for a generation."

What's actually finished

The maritime structure, residential buildings and most of the eco-district's core infrastructure were delivered by the December 2024 inauguration, with residences continuing to be handed over through 2025 and 2026. Public spaces — the promenade, the park, and the marina's final commercial phase — are being completed in stages through 2027.

For buyers, most Mareterra units were reserved before completion, and remaining availability now runs largely through private, off-market channels rather than public listings.
