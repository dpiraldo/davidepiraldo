Grey list update — where things stand in June 2026

Davide Piraldo·July 2026·4 min read
editorial photograph — FATF plenary session, Paris

At its June 2026 plenary in Paris, the FATF confirmed Monaco remains on its list of jurisdictions under increased monitoring — but the language used marks real, measurable progress toward an exit.

The timeline so far

  • June 2024
    Monaco placed under increased monitoring following a MONEYVAL evaluation citing uneven enforcement effectiveness.
  • April 2025
    Monaco's government introduces a National Strategy and Action Plan for 2025–2027, targeting five priority reform areas.
  • December 2024
    MONEYVAL re-rates Monaco as compliant or largely compliant on 39 of the FATF's 40 technical recommendations.
  • June 2025
    Joint FATF-MONEYVAL plenary assesses Monaco's progress as "broadly addressed."
  • June 2026
    FATF's fourth progress report review concludes Monaco has "substantially completed" its action plan and now "warrants an on-site assessment."

"A near-perfect technical scorecard hasn't been enough on its own — the FATF wants to see the reforms working in practice, not just written into law."

What's left

The remaining hurdle isn't legislative — it's evidentiary. The FATF wants to see reforms functioning as sustained, everyday practice: completed prosecutions, dissuasive sanctions actually applied, and successful seizures of criminal assets, reported consistently over time. An on-site visit — the date for which has not yet been announced — will assess whether that evidentiary bar has been cleared.

What this means for buyers today

As of this writing, Monaco's presence on the grey list has not disrupted international buyer flows, transaction volumes, or price dynamics in the Principality's real estate market. The exit process depends on demonstrated enforcement effectiveness over time, not further legislation — meaning a specific exit date remains unconfirmed, even as the underlying compliance framework is now considered largely complete.
