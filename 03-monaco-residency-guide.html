Monaco residency: requirements in 2026

Davide Piraldo·July 2026·6 min read
editorial photograph — Monaco government quarter

Buying an apartment in Monaco and becoming a resident are two separate processes. One is a real estate transaction. The other is an administrative application with its own timeline, its own documentation, and its own gatekeepers.

Monaco runs no residency-by-investment or golden-visa programme. There is no fixed net-worth threshold in law. What the Principality asks for instead is proof that you can support yourself indefinitely without local employment.

The three pillars

  • Accommodation. A genuine home in Monaco — either owned or leased for at least 12 months — sized appropriately for the applicant and any dependents. A hotel room does not qualify.
  • Financial self-sufficiency. Demonstrated through a Monaco bank account. There is no statutory minimum, but market convention among Monaco banks sits at approximately €500,000, sometimes higher depending on the institution and applicant profile.
  • Clean criminal record. A recent extract, apostilled and translated by a sworn translator, from every country of residence in the past five years.

Non-EU applicants: one extra step

Monaco does not issue its own entry visas. Nationals outside the EU/EEA/Switzerland must first obtain a French long-stay Type-D visa from a French consulate before applying for the Monegasque Carte de Séjour — a process run entirely by France, with its own separate timeline.

"Renting is the fastest route into residency. Buying strengthens the application — it doesn't replace any of the requirements."

The three card tiers

The first card issued is a Carte de Résident Temporaire, valid one year and renewable. After three years of genuine residence, applicants can move to an ordinary card valid for three years; after ten years, to a privileged card valid for ten. None of these transitions are automatic — each is a fresh application and reassessment.

One detail catches many applicants off guard: residency and tax residency are not the same status. To access the zero-income-tax regime, an individual needs the Carte de Séjour and a separate tax residency certificate, generally requiring more than 183 days physically spent in Monaco each year.
